Description

Feseekh is a traditional Egyptian dish made from fermented and salted fish, typically mullet fish. It is often served during the Sham El-Nessim holiday, which marks the beginning of spring in Egypt. The fish is salted and left to ferment for several weeks, resulting in a strong, pungent flavor. Feseekh is usually accompanied by side dishes such as onions, lettuce, and Egyptian bread. It is a dish that evokes strong opinions, with many people loving its unique taste, while others find it too strong or unappealing. The preparation and consumption of feseekh is deeply rooted in Egyptian culture and tradition.

Ingredients

  • Salted mullet fish (or other types of fish)
  • Onions
  • Lettuce
  • Egyptian bread (baladi bread)
  • Vegetable oil (optional)
  • Lemon wedges (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the salted fish by rinsing it under cold water to remove excess salt.
  2. Place the fish in a serving dish and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to develop.
  3. Slice the onions and arrange them around the fish.
  4. Wash and tear the lettuce leaves, then place them on the side.
  5. Serve the feseekh with Egyptian bread, allowing everyone to take pieces of bread and enjoy the fish with the onions and lettuce.
  6. Optionally, drizzle some vegetable oil over the fish and serve with lemon wedges for added flavor.
  7. Enjoy the feseekh with family and friends, celebrating the tradition of Sham El-Nessim.
