Description

Molokhia, also known as jute mallow or Jew's mallow, refers to the leaves of the Corchorus olitorius plant, which are used as a vegetable. It's a popular ingredient in Middle Eastern and African cuisine, particularly in Egypt, where it's often cooked into a stew or soup. The leaves can be used fresh or dried, and the dish is known for its unique, slightly viscous texture and earthy, herby flavor.

Ingredients

  • Molokhia leaves (fresh or dried)
  • Garlic
  • Coriander
  • Chicken or rabbit (optional)
  • Chicken broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon juice (optional)

steps

  1. If using fresh molokhia, wash and chop the leaves finely. If using dried molokhia, soak it in water for a few hours until rehydrated.
  2. In a pot, heat some oil and saut√© minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped molokhia leaves to the pot and stir well.
  4. Pour in chicken broth or water, and bring to a simmer. If using meat, add it at this stage.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and let it cook for about 20-30 minutes until the leaves are tender and the flavors meld together.
  6. Optionally, add lemon juice before serving for extra flavor.
  7. Serve hot with rice or bread.
