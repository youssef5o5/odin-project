ma74y

Description

The word “mahshi” in Arabic literally means “stuffed.” When this word is used on its own, it generally refers to an assortment of vegetables that have been stuffed with seasoned Egyptian short-grain rice and cooked in broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 1 large zucchini
  • 1 large tomato
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 cup Egyptian short-grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried mint
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger

steps

  1. Wash the vegetables thoroughly. Cut the tops off the eggplant, zucchini, and tomato, and scoop out the insides using a small spoon or a melon baller, leaving a thin wall of flesh.
  2. In a bowl, combine the rice, tomato paste, salt, pepper, olive oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, dried mint, dried thyme, ground cinnamon, allspice, ground black pepper, ground turmeric, and ground ginger. Mix well.
  3. Stuff each vegetable with the rice mixture until they are filled but not packed too tightly.
  4. In a large pot, arrange the stuffed vegetables upright. Pour the vegetable broth or water over them until they are almost covered.
  5. Cover the pot and bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and the rice is cooked through.
  6. Serve hot with a side of yogurt or salad.
